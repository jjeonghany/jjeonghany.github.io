Jeonghan Yu, Ph.D.

I am a postdoctoral research scientist in Mechanical Engineering at Columbia University. My research focuses on wearable and rehabilitation robotics, human movement analysis, mechanism design, and data-driven approaches for understanding and assisting human motion.

I received my B.S. and combined M.S./Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Seoul National University. My broader research goal is to develop human-centered robotic systems that integrate mechanical design, control, biomechanics, and computational intelligence.

Research

Wearable and Rehabilitation Robotics

I design and evaluate robotic systems that physically interact with the human body, with a focus on effective assistance, adaptability, and human-specific motor behavior.

Human Movement Analysis

I analyze whole-body kinematics, ground reaction forces, center of pressure, and intersegmental coordination to understand how humans organize movement and stability during complex motor tasks.

Design Intelligence

I am interested in computational and data-driven methods for robotic mechanism design, including optimization, synthesis, and learning-based design frameworks.
