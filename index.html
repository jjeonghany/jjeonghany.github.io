Who I am

I am a postdoctoral research scientist in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at Columbia University, working with Prof. Sunil K. Agrawal. My research centers on wearable and rehabilitation robotics, biomechanical analysis of whole-body movement, and robotic training systems for individuals with neurological injuries.

I received my Ph.D. in Mechanical Engineering from Seoul National University, advised by Prof. Yoon Young Kim. My doctoral research focused on topology optimization and autonomous synthesis of self-aligning wearable exoskeleton mechanisms. Across my work, I aim to connect mechanical design intelligence with physically effective, human-specific robotic assistance.

Research

Wearable and Rehabilitation Robotics

Design, control, and experimental evaluation of exoskeletons and cable-driven robotic systems for gait assistance, balance support, standing, sitting, and functional upper-body activities.

Human Movement and Biomechanics

Motion-capture, ground-reaction-force, EMG, and center-of-pressure analysis to understand movement coordination, postural stability, and human-specific assistance needs.

Mechanism Design Intelligence

Topology optimization, shape optimization, sensitivity analysis, and nonlinear inverse design for linkage mechanisms and adaptive wearable robotic joints.

Control and Human-Robot Interaction

Adaptive impedance control, model predictive control, real-time motor control, and AI-driven assistance strategies for physical human-robot interaction.
